# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User

**Primary**: Founder/VP Engineering at $1-50M ARR startups

**Characteristics**:
- Uses 3-5 AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot standard)
- Spends $200-2k/month on AI (median $400-600/month)
- Manages technical team (2-15 engineers)
- Is frugal with burn rate (every dollar matters)
- Uncomfortable with untracked spend

**Why**: High spend → high savings potential → motivated to switch

---

## Where They Hang Out

1. **Twitter/X** (60% distribution)
   - Founders follow @levelsio, @paulg, AI accounts
   - Threads about "overpriced tools" get 500-2k likes
   - DM friendly, respond to cold reaches

2. **Hacker News** (20% distribution)
   - "Show HN" post for audit tool (high viral potential)
   - Comments on AI cost threads link to our tool
   - Niche but high-intent audience

3. **Slack Communities** (15% distribution)
   - Indie Hackers Slack (#founders channel)
   - YC Founders group (if accessible)
   - Techstars alumni Slack

4. **Product Hunt** (5% distribution)
   - Launch Day: aim for Top 5 trending
   - Free product + founder story = high upvotes

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 budget)

### Week 1: Cold Outreach (30 users)
- **Channel**: Twitter DMs + cold email
- **Message**: "Just built a free tool to audit your AI spend. See if you're overspending on Claude/ChatGPT. 2-min check."
- **Target**: 20 founders + 10 tech reporters
- **Expected Response Rate**: 15% (3 per day from 20 outreaches)

### Week 2: Show HN (40 users)
- **Post on HN**: "Show HN: Free AI spend audit tool (find hidden savings)"
- **Time**: Wednesday 8 AM PST (peak traffic)
- **Strategy**: Help comments, answer questions, collect feedback
- **Expected**: 30-50 visits, 5-10 sign-ups if on front page

### Week 3: Slack/Community (20 users)
- **Indie Hackers Slack**: Post in #projects
- **YC Founders Slack**: If access, reply to AI cost questions
- **Reddit**: r/startups, r/Entrepreneur (3-5 posts max, no spam)
- **Expected**: 15-20 users from communities

### Week 4: Content + Referral (10 users)
- **Content**: Tweet thread "Why are founders overspending $X/month on AI tools?"
- **Referral**: Ask early users to share with founders group
- **Expected**: 5-10 viral referrals

**Total**: 30 + 40 + 20 + 10 = **100 users** (conservative estimate)

## Unfair Advantage

1. **First-Mover** in horizontal AI spend audits
   - Competitors exist for specific tools (e.g., Copilot team plans) but not cross-tool
   - First to product = brand equity

2. **Credex Connection**
   - Lead magnet for discounted credits business
   - Direct path to monetization (high-value leads)
   - Other tools can't offer credit arbitrage

3. **Founder Credibility**
   - Built by engineers who *actually* use these tools
   - Transparent pricing + reasoning (not a black box)
   - Community-driven (open to feedback)

4. **Trust-First Approach**
   - Show savings even when $0 (builds trust)
   - No fabricated recommendations (vs clickbait competitors)
   - Disclose all AI usage (REFLECTION.md)

## Week 2 Pivots (if needed)

- **Low adoption (<50 audits)**: Pivot to "most expensive tool" audit (e.g., just ChatGPT Enterprise → alternatives)
- **Low email capture (<10%)**: Make capture optional; offer shareable results instead
- **High bounce rate**: Simplify form (start with single tool, add others)
- **Bot spam**: Increase honeypot sophistication or add rate limiting
