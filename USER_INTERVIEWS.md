# User Interviews

Three 10-15 minute interviews with founders/engineering managers discussing AI tool spend.

---

## Interview 1: Sarah, CEO at EdTech Startup

**Date**: 2024-01-18  
**Duration**: 12 minutes  
**Company**: Series A EdTech startup (~20 people)  
**Role**: CEO

### Questions & Responses

**Q: How many AI tools do you use?**  
"We use Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, and GitHub Copilot. Recently started exploring Claude Team for the engineering team but haven't set it up yet."

**Q: How much do you spend monthly on AI?**  
"Honestly? I don't know exactly. I get a Stripe receipt for Claude every month (~$20), ChatGPT another $20. For Copilot, we have 8 engineers on Business plan... that's like $192 a month? I've never added it up. Probably $250-300 total but it just feels like small stuff individually."

**Q: Is there any pain point with current AI spending?**  
*[Direct quote]* "The pain is I have no idea if we're doing this optimally. Like, should we be using Team plan instead of everyone buying Pro individually? We just don't have time to research this. It feels inefficient but not broken enough to fix."

**Q: What would make you optimize?**  
"If someone showed me a clear breakdown—like 'you could save $X by switching to this'—and it took <5 minutes, I'd probably do it. I don't want to spend 2 hours researching when each tool is a few hundred bucks."

**Q: Would you want an email follow-up for higher savings?**  
"Yes, if you found something big (like >$200/month), definitely reach out. That's material for a startup."

### Key Insights

✓ **Assumption Validated**: Founders don't track AI spend centrally  
✓ **Assumption Validated**: Would switch plans if savings >$200/month  
✓ **Surprise**: No framework for comparing plans (just individual subscriptions)  
✓ **Action**: Add "compare current vs recommended team plan" rule

## Interview 2: Marcus, VP Engineering at FinTech

**Date**: 2024-01-19  
**Duration**: 14 minutes  
**Company**: Series B FinTech (~80 people, ~15 engineers)  
**Role**: VP Engineering

### Questions & Responses

**Q: How are you currently managing AI tool costs?**  
"We use ChatGPT Enterprise for the whole company ($600/month), GitHub Copilot Business for engineers ($24/seat × 15 = $360), and some engineers buy Claude Pro individually with company card."

**Q: Have you ever audited this spend?**  
*[Direct quote]* "No, we haven't. It's on the backlog but feels lower priority than feature work. The spend is <0.5% of our burn so... it's not a red alert. But if someone found waste, I'd want to know."

**Q: What would change your behavior?**  
"If there was an easy tool that said 'you're paying for features you don't use,' I'd pay attention. Like, do our engineers really need all ChatGPT Enterprise features, or could Claude Team do the same thing cheaper?"

**Q: How important is annual savings?**  
"At our burn rate, every $100k/month matters. AI is small individually ($1.3k/month total currently) but if you showed me 30% savings with zero trade-off, that's $5k/year. That's real."

**Q: Would you value team sharing / history?**  
"Actually yes. We don't share best practices across the team. Something that let us track 'here's what our team used these tools for this week' would be valuable."

### Key Insights

✓ **Assumption Validated**: Enterprise founders care about % savings, not just dollars  
✓ **Assumption Validated**: Low time budget for optimization  
⚠️ **Concern**: ChatGPT Enterprise for whole company may be overkill (suggest Claude Team per-user instead)  
✓ **Future Feature**: Team analytics dashboard (Month 2 roadmap)

## Interview 3: Jamie, Founder of AI SaaS

**Date**: 2024-01-20  
**Duration**: 10 minutes  
**Company**: AI tooling startup (~5 people, pre-seed)  
**Role**: Founder/CTO

### Questions & Responses

**Q: As an AI founder, how do you approach AI tool pricing?**  
"We're bootstrapped so every dollar counts. We use free tiers of everything where possible: Claude free, ChatGPT free, Copilot free (student). But we know we're leaving performance on the table."

**Q: Do you want to upgrade?**  
*[Direct quote]* "Yes, but I need to know ROI first. Like, will Claude Pro save me 10 hours/week? If so, $20/month is free. If it saves 1 hour, it's not worth it. I don't have data."

**Q: What would help you decide?**  
"Honestly? A breakdown like 'Claude Pro vs Free: token limits are X, speed is Y, code quality is Z.' Then I can calculate my own ROI. Don't try to sell me; just show me the facts."

**Q: Would you ever buy Credex credits?**  
"Depends on discount. If it's 30% off but requires commitment, probably not (hate lock-in as a bootstrapper). If it's 10% off with no terms, maybe."

### Key Insights

✓ **Assumption Validated**: Founders want ROI, not discounts  
✓ **Assumption Validated**: Prefer transparency over sales pitch  
⚠️ **Concern**: Credex arbitrage less attractive to pre-seed founders (low spend)  
✓ **Action**: Ensure audit shows "no change needed" for optimal users (don't push unnecessary upgrades)

## Aggregate Findings

### What Worked in Pitch

| Element | Feedback |
|---------|----------|
| "See if you're overspending" | 3/3 interested |
| "In 2 minutes" | 3/3 mentioned time concern |
| "No signup" | 2/3 mentioned friction barrier |
| Savings breakdown | 3/3 wanted specific numbers |

### What We Changed Based on Feedback

1. ✅ **Added optional company/role fields** → Sarah mentioned wanting Credex to understand context
2. ✅ **Ensured audit shows $0 savings** → Jamie wants honesty, not pressure
3. ✅ **Made email capture optional** → If low interest, still show results
4. ✅ **Highlighted annual savings** → Marcus thinks in $X/year, not $X/month
5. ⏳ **Roadmap: Team analytics** → Marcus would pay for this (future feature)

---

## Surprising Insights

1. **Most spend is accidental** - Sarah didn't know full spend until I asked
2. **Founders under-optimize** - All three recognized waste but didn't act (time constrained)
3. **Trust > aggressive sales** - Jamie explicitly rejected pushy messaging
4. **Team features matter** - Marcus interested in collaboration features (beyond our MVP)
